## Summary

Patent US11747438 relates to a cognitive electronic warfare scheduling system for allocating electronic warfare sensors and/or receivers. It specifically describes a cognitive electronic warfare scheduler and process for allocating receiver assets dynamically in real-time in response to detected signals.

## Key Concepts

The patent describes assigning priority levels by:
- Determining a threat level of each active emitter in the theater
- Assigning a relative priority to the plurality of frequency bands according to the threat level of the emitters generating EM signals in each band
- Performing each step continuously and in near real-time

The system focuses on dynamic allocation of receiver assets based on cognitive analysis of the electromagnetic environment and threat assessment.

## Technical Field

Electronic warfare systems, specifically cognitive systems for dynamic resource allocation and receiver asset management in response to detected electromagnetic signals.

## Applications

The patent relates to electronic warfare scheduling and allocation of sensing resources for defense applications, focusing on intelligent and adaptive allocation of receiver assets in response to threat environments.
